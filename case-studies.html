The Book of Sounds — UX case study for toddlers

Designing a safe, simple and character-focused sound discovery app for very young children.

AProblem

Many children face barriers when accessing safe, high-quality educational apps. The project focused on four main concerns: advertising, accidental interactions, one-size-fits-all design for young users, and the lack of visibility for parents about what their children are learning.

BProduct decisions

  • Separate child and parent areas.
  • Keep advertising away from the child-facing experience.
  • Use large, colorful characters as the main focus.
  • Reduce UI noise and distractions.
  • Create simple flows for very young users.
  • Support learning through discovery, reinforcement and test modes.

DUser Testing & Learnings

Testing validated that navigation was easy and onboarding was quick to understand, but also revealed areas to improve: onboarding subtitles were not engaging enough and the parental help system needed to become more interactive.

EOutcome

The app was released for free on Google Play and reached more than 10,000 downloads. Although it was not designed as a monetized product, it became a valuable learning project around UX, publishing, kids apps and real mobile users.
